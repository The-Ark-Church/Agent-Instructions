# The Ark Church — Communications Style Guide Summary

*Based on the Ark Church Communications Guide, Revised June 2024. Based on AP Stylebook.*

---

## Communication Values

- **Advocate for the audience** — make people's lives easier, not more difficult
- **Communicate with excellence** — if it's worth saying, get it right
- **Be user-friendly** — answer who, what, when, where, why, and how; clear beats clever
- **Avoid insider lingo** — explain or avoid church jargon
- **Say less to communicate more** — keep it brief
- **Don't sell** — inform, cast vision, share experience; describe real outcomes
- **Communicate what we want *for* people, not *from* them** — cast vision, not to-dos
- **Be dependable** — consistent messages, style, and channels
- **Let personality shine** — authentic, informal, sincere, positive, fun

---

## Best Practices

- Always get a second set of eyes on it (required, not optional)
- Have a clear goal before writing anything
- Know your audience; write from their perspective
- Lead with the most important thing (inverted pyramid)
- Don't overload — limit to one message or one first step
- Choose the right channel for the message
- Cut, cut, cut — eliminate without losing meaning
- Use active voice ("Thousands attended" not "The event was attended by thousands")
- Avoid overused/tired phrases

## Times

- **Format:** `6:00pm`, `7:00-8:30am`, `8:00am-5:00pm`, `10:00 and 11:30am`
- Always include numbers after the colon (do `8:00`, not `8`)
- `am`/`pm` — lowercase, no periods, no spaces between number and letters
- Use a hyphen with no spaces for ranges: `8:00-9:30pm`
- Use `noon` or `midnight`, not `12:00am` or `12:00pm`
- Avoid redundancies: don't say "8:00am in the morning"
- Use singular day names for events: `Wednesday at 7:00pm` (not "Wednesdays")
- Use Oxford commas in time lists: `Sunday at 9:30, 11:30am, 1:00, and 6:00pm`

---

## Dates

- **Format:** `Tuesday, April 28`
- Day number only — no ordinal suffixes (`28`, not `28th`)
- Include the day of the week so people don't have to look it up
- Replace "from" with a comma; replace "to" with a hyphen
  - Do: `Thursday, April 3, 8:00-9:30pm`
  - Don't: `Thursday from 8:00 to 9:30pm`
- Do not abbreviate days or months (except in character-limited spaces like SMS/Twitter)
- Drop the year unless needed
- Multi-day: `May 31 - June 1`

---

## Numbers

- Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above
  - Exception: always use numerals for ages
- Spell out numbers that start a sentence (or restructure the sentence)
- **Ages:** Always numerals; hyphenate age adjectives (`a 5-year-old boy`); never use apostrophes (`30s`, not `30's`)
- **Grades:** Use numerals with ordinal suffix (`5th grade`, `9th-12th grade`)

---

## Titles

- **Italics** for: series titles, stand-alone message titles, podcast titles, Bible plans, movie titles, publications (books, magazines, newspapers)
- **Quotation marks** for: components of a larger work (a message title within a series, a podcast episode)

---

## Capitalization

- No all caps — it's the written equivalent of yelling
- Only capitalize proper nouns
- Seasons: lowercase (`spring`, `fall`) unless part of a proper noun (`Winter Carnival`)
- **Headlines:** Title case — capitalize nouns and verbs; lowercase prepositions, articles, conjunctions (unless first word)
- **Formal titles:** Capitalize before a name (`Executive Pastor Phillip Moore`); lowercase when used generically (`the senior pastor`)
- **Religious terms:**
  - Capitalize: `Bible`, `Scripture`, `God's Word`
  - Lowercase: `biblical`, `scriptural`, `godly`
  - Capitalize pronouns referring to God: `He`, `Him`, `His`, `You`, `Your`
    - Exception: when quoting a Scripture translation that doesn't capitalize them

---

## Formatting

- Use **bold** or *italics* to emphasize; underlining only for hyperlinks
- Bullets: consistent style and indent spacing throughout a document
- Be consistent within a list about whether bullet items end with punctuation

---

## Social Media

- **Handles:** Use the official handle exactly as listed, including capitalization (`@thearkchurch` on Instagram is always lowercase)
- **Hashtags:** Lowercase preferred; title case only if it aids readability (`#arkchurch`, `#BaptismWeek`)

---

## Quoting Scripture

- Do not abbreviate books of the Bible (`Galatians`, not `Gal.`)
- Italicize Bible verses when possible
- Always include version reference (`NIV`, `KJV`, etc.)
- Use an ellipsis (`…`) when omitting text
- Use quotation marks when they are part of the original text or when quoting Scripture within a paragraph

## Punctuation

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Exclamation points | No doubles (`!!`) or combos (`?!`); don't use in consecutive sentences |
| Sentence spacing | One space between sentences, not two |
| Commas | Oxford comma in lists of three or more: `games, food, and prizes` |
| Quotation marks | For spoken words, reference to the word itself, or irony — not to soften meaning |
| Punctuation in quotes | Periods and commas go *inside* quotes; dash, semicolon, `?`, `!` go inside only if they apply to the quote alone |
| Hyphens (`-`) | For hyphenated words, times, date ranges, phone numbers |
| Dashes (`–`) | Within sentences for emphasis; no spaces on either side |
| Apostrophes | Not in plurals (`FAQs`, `30s`, `DVDs`) |
| Colons | Only after a complete sentence; use to clarify or expand |
| Semicolons | Separate two closely related independent clauses |
| Parentheses | Use sparingly; prefer commas or dashes |
| Brackets | Rarer still — for parenthetical inside a parenthetical, or to add clarity to a quote |
| Ampersands | Use "and" unless space is limited or it's part of a proper name |

## Grammar Notes

- Fix fragments
- Subject-verb agreement — look at the subject of the prepositional phrase, not the object
- Ending sentences with a preposition is okay unless it's unnecessary (`Where are they?` not `Where are they at?`)
- Use correct prepositions: "information about" (not "information on"); "questions about" (not "questions around")
- Contractions are fine — casual style is preferred
- Hyphenate compound modifiers unless the first word ends in `-ly` (`age-appropriate`, `culturally relevant`)

- Addresses always lowercase, never hyphenated for line breaks

## Websites & Links

- Avoid "click on" or "click here" — make the subject the link
  - Do: `Register for Legacy Foundation`
  - Don't: `Click here to register for Legacy Foundation`
- `internet` — lowercase
- `website` — one word
- `Web` — capitalize only as shorthand for World Wide Web
- `online` — one word (not `on-line`)
- URLs: never hyphenate for line breaks; do not capitalize; don't end a sentence with a URL followed by punctuation
- Use `www.thearkchurch.com` (with `www`) when it's a destination in print
- Always hyperlink URLs in email and social media
